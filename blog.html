The Book Of Secrets
osho

"The Book of Secrets" by Osho, originally published as "Vigyan Bhairav Tantra," is a spiritual and philosophical text that explores various meditation techniques and practices. Osho, a Indian mystic and spiritual teacher, provided his commentary on these ancient tantric texts, offering insights and guidance on how to attain self-realization and spiritual enlightenment.

The book consists of 112 different meditation techniques, each designed to help individuals go beyond their ordinary, everyday consciousness and experience a deeper connection with their inner selves and the universe. These techniques cover a wide range of approaches, from breath control and mantra meditation to visualization and contemplation of various aspects of existence.

Harry Potter
J K Rowlling

Harry Potter is a series of seven fantasy novels written by British author J. K. Rowling. The novels chronicle the lives of a young wizard, Harry Potter, and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, all of whom are students at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The main story arc concerns Harry's conflict with Lord Voldemort, a dark wizard who intends to become immortal, overthrow the wizard governing body known as the Ministry of Magic and subjugate all wizards and Muggles (non-magical people).

A series of many genres (which includes elements of mystery, thriller, adventure, horror, and romance), including fantasy, drama, coming-of-age fiction, and the British school story, the world of Harry Potter explores numerous themes and includes many cultural meanings and references.

Train To Pakistan
khushwant singh

Train to Pakistan is a historical novel by writer Khushwant Singh, published in 1956. It recounts the Partition of India in August 1947 through the perspective of Mano Majra, a fictional border village.

Instead of depicting the Partition in terms of only the political events surrounding it, Khushwant Singh digs into a deep local focus, providing a human dimension which brings to the event a sense of reality, horror, and believability.
